"Art of Lifting Heavy"

by Michael Parker

Lifting heavy isn't just about the amount you can move. You see, lifting heavy is a form of art. It takes years to master some of these lifts such as squats, deadlifts, or cleans. You know I can remember my first time squatting heavy.

I was 145lbs at the time attempting a 225lb squat at Western Oregon University's gym. Thing is to someone of my frame that's a big deal. Bar loaded, I take my position, my feet space themselves out into the perfect shoulder width and my hands somehow have a grip on the bar death couldn't even handle. My body was ready but my mind, mentally I had a block.

With a swift slap to my ass my partner yells, "Form looks great, let's go. You got this!". I snap out of it and unrack while taking in a massive breath. Shacking all over I engage every muscle fiber, step back, and go ass to grass. To me the weight felt unbearable and when I hit the bottom my legs wanted to give out and explode. Everyone's eyes were on me at this point, and that's when my pride kicked in and my body found the strength. Face super red because I forgot to let my breath out on the way up, I successfully rack and hear cheering while I proceed to receive more slaps to my ass in victory.


That was the last day of a 60 day training program doing a classic routine, The 5 X 5. I hit all my goals and more. I started out 135 lbs and ended 145 lbs with a ~135 lb overall average increase to my max weight per lift. My starting & ending stats I've posted below:

Now let's dive into how the 5 x 5 routine works. You have 2 sets of workout cards: A & B

5 x 5 Workout A
Squats Sets: 5 Reps: 5 Weight: 45 lbs
Bench Sets: 5 Reps: 5 Weight: 45 lbs
Landmine Press Sets: 5 Reps: 5 Weight: 45 lbs

5 x 5 Workout B
Squats Sets: 5 Reps: 5 Weight: 45 lbs
Barbell Rows Sets: 5 Reps: 5 Weight: 45 lbs
Deadlift Sets: 1 Reps: 5 Weight: 65 lbs

This is an example of a beginner lifters card set. You'll notice deadlift is the only lift with 1 set. It can be very challenging for a beginner lifter to squat and deadlift in the same day while each week increasing weight. The landmine press can be replaced with overhead press. Which the overhead press is usually the standard for this routine; however, if you're someone with a shoulder injury or diagnosed problem than I'd suggest the landmine press. The same muscles will be worked but the movement is safer on our rotator cuffs.
